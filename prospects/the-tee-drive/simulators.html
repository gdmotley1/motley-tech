[ // 02 ] THE TRACKING

What gets measured gets better.

Every shot logs ball-flight and club-flight data in real time. Same tracking standard used on the PGA Tour. Eight of the most useful below. Twelve bays of nuance below that.

01 / RAW POWER
BALL SPEED
How fast the ball leaves the face, in mph. The single biggest predictor of distance.
~168 MPH · TOUR AVG
02 / ENGINE
CLUB SPEED
How fast the club head is moving at impact. The input. Everything else is the output.
~113 MPH · TOUR AVG
03 / EFFICIENCY
SMASH FACTOR
Ball speed over club speed. How well you transferred energy. 1.50 is elite. 1.30 means you missed center.
1.50 · ELITE
04 / TRAJECTORY
LAUNCH ANGLE
Degrees up. Low launch with high spin equals carry. High launch with low spin equals roll.
10–14° · OPTIMAL DRIVER
05 / LIFT
SPIN RATE
RPM around the ball axis. Too much, you balloon. Too little, you can't hold a green.
2,500–2,800 · DRIVER
06 / SHAPE
SPIN AXIS
Tilt of the spin axis in degrees. Positive numbers fade. Negative draw. Now you know which is which.
±5° · STRAIGHT
07 / DIRECTION
CLUB PATH
In-to-out or out-to-in, in degrees. The cause of slices, hooks, and the shot you actually meant to hit.
0° · NEUTRAL
08 / AIM
FACE ANGLE
Where the face was actually pointing at impact, relative to your target. Spoiler: usually not where you thought.
±1° · TOUR LEVEL

Plus: carry, total, apex, descent angle, attack angle, dynamic loft, spin loft, swing direction, low point, swing plane, and a dozen more. Toggle on what you want, ignore the rest.
