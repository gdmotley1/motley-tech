[ // 01 ] THESIS

Why this exists.

The closest tour-grade simulator to Milledgeville is a ninety-minute drive to Atlanta. The closest indoor range that runs year-round is even farther. For a town that loves golf and has nowhere to play it nine months out of the year, that's a problem.

Georgia summers are 95° and humid. Spring is rain-delayed half the time. Winter days are over by 5:30. Add work, family, the price of green fees, the four-hour round, the drive to a course, the bag of clubs you have to keep in your trunk, and the average serious golfer in Baldwin County plays a fraction of the rounds they want to.

The Tee Drive is the answer. Three bays, two blocks from the square, open until midnight five nights a week. Tour-grade ball-flight tracking on every shot. Three hundred world-famous courses in the library. A real bar. The same gear PGA pros use to practice at home, sitting on Hancock Street.

[ // 03 ] THE TECH

Powered by passion. Driven by tech.

Industry-grade launch monitors track twenty-seven ball- and club-flight parameters in real time. The same standard the PGA Tour uses to dial in their bags. Auto-scoring dart cameras catch every throw, run live brackets across all three bays, and post results to leaderboards mounted in the bar.

None of this exists to look impressive. It exists because deliberate practice (Anders Ericsson, Psychological Review 1993) is the foundational principle behind every player who's ever gotten meaningfully better at anything: immediate feedback, on specific performance parameters, in a controlled, repeatable environment.

That's the definition of a simulator bay. It's also what a public range fails at on every count.
