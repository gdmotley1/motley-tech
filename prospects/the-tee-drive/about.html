About

For the love of the game.

A simulator bar built by people who think a great round should not require a free afternoon, a trailer hitch, or a ninety-minute drive.

Why this exists

Milledgeville loves golf. The calendar disagrees.

The closest tour-grade simulator is a long drive up the highway. Georgia summers hit 95 and humid, spring is rain-delayed half the time, and winter light is gone by 5:30. Add work, family, green fees, and the four-hour round, and most serious golfers around Baldwin County play a fraction of what they want to.

The Tee Drive is the answer. Four bays, two blocks from the square. Tour-grade ball tracking on every shot and three hundred world-famous courses in the library. Two dart sims, a pool table, a console lounge, and a real bar, so the night does not end when the round does.

Rain or shine, January or July, the course is always open.

The space

More than bays.

Half the floor is for swinging. The other half is for everyone else: a pool table, a console lounge with Xbox, PS5, Switch, and Wii, and a bar pouring cold beer and real cocktails with your tab tracked to wherever you're sitting.

The table

Racked and waiting between rounds.

The lounge

Xbox, PS5, Switch, and Wii on the big screens.

The 19th hole

Cold beer, real cocktails, no plastic cups.

The tech

Powered by passion. Driven by tech.

Industry-grade launch monitors read twenty-seven ball- and club-flight parameters in real time, the same standard tour players use to dial in their bags. The dart sims score themselves with overhead cameras and post results to leaderboards in the bar.

None of it is there to look impressive. Immediate feedback on every swing, in a controlled environment you can come back to every week, is simply the fastest way anyone gets better at anything. That is what a bay gives you that a public range never will.
